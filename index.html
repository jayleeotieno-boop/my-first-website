The Formula 1 Website

If you’re new to Formula 1, think of it as the absolute pinnacle of motorsport, where twenty of the world’s best drivers race custom-built, open-wheel cars at breathtaking speeds—often over 200 miles per hour. Unlike regular racing, F1 isn’t just about who has the fastest car or the bravest foot on the throttle; it’s a high-stakes blend of cutting-edge engineering, razor-sharp strategy, and human endurance. Each Grand Prix weekend, held on iconic circuits from the streets of Monaco to the high-speed banks of Monza, combines a nail-biting qualifying session (where drivers fight for the best starting position) with a Sunday race that typically lasts around 90 minutes. Overtakes, pit stops measured in under two seconds, and ever-changing weather can flip the race on its head in an instant, and with only ten teams (each with two drivers) competing for two world titles—Drivers’ and Constructors’—every single point matters. In short, F1 is a thrilling, globe-trotting chess match played out at bullet-train speeds, and once you grasp the basics, it’s nearly impossible to look away.
